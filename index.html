Personal Hobbies and Experiences

A Calling I Never Expected

Teaching was never the dream I once held close to my heart. If I am completely honest, it was not the path I wanted. It was simply the direction life led me to. But becoming a student teacher changed everything.

At first, I was unsure. I doubted myself. I questioned whether I truly belonged in front of a classroom. Yet as the days passed, I slowly began to understand something beautiful. Teaching is not just about instructing it is about connecting.

Even though I am the teacher, I learn from my students every single day. I learn about their struggles, their silent battles, their dreams, and their fears. Some of them share stories they cannot even tell their closest friends. They talk to me about relationships, family problems, financial hardships, and personal challenges. In those moments, I realized that my role is not just to teach lessons from a book, but to become a safe space.

And that is when I started to love teaching.

Growing up, books were once my entire world. In high school, I was a super fan of reading e-books, especially on Wattpad. I would stay up all night reading romance, drama, fantasy until my eyes hurt. Maybe that‚Äôs even the reason I now have graded eyesight. But through those stories, I learned so much. I improved my spelling, discovered new words, understood deeper meanings, and expanded my imagination. Books taught me in ways school sometimes could not.

But now, things are different.

I buy books with good intentions, telling myself I will read them again like before. Yet I cannot even finish the first page. Instead of staying up all night reading, I now choose sleep. Life feels heavier. Responsibilities feel real. Maybe I am still trying to find my rhythm again.

I am not an athlete. I do not have championship titles or sports achievements. But maybe one day, I want to learn tennis. Maybe it‚Äôs never too late to start something new.

I love traveling not just traveling but going on adventures. I crave experiences that challenge me, that push me out of my comfort zone. I may not have the kind of experiences others proudly share. I am still figuring things out. I am still learning how to succeed in life, how to build stability, how to grow.

And maybe that‚Äôs okay.

Because teaching the career I never wanted is slowly shaping me into the person I am becoming. Through my students, I am learning empathy, strength, patience, and resilience. Through their stories, I see pieces of my own journey.

I may still be figuring out life.

But one thing I know now I did not choose teaching. Teaching chose me. And somewhere between lesson plans and heartfelt conversations, I found purpose.

Philosophy

My Philosophy of Education üìö

To me, education is more than a system, it is a responsibility and a calling. I believe that teaching is one of the most powerful ways to shape the future of a nation. Every learner who enters the classroom carries potential, and it is my role to help uncover, nurture, and guide that potential toward growth and purpose.

I see education as a space where knowledge meets character. Academic excellence is important, but it must walk hand in hand with integrity, empathy, and resilience. A truly meaningful education forms not only intelligent minds but also compassionate hearts.

My approach to teaching centers on clarity, flexibility, and inclusion. I recognize that no two learners are the same. Each student has unique strengths, needs, and ways of understanding the world. Through inclusive practices, differentiated strategies, and learner-centered instruction, I strive to create an environment where everyone feels seen, supported, and capable of success.

I believe classrooms should be safe spaces for curiosity, respectful dialogue, and discovery, places where diversity is embraced and every voice matters. When learners feel valued, they gain confidence not only to achieve academically but also to contribute meaningfully to society.
